The Catalog Problem

How traditional stationery commerce structures fail at scale

⚠️

Variant Explosion

Creating separate products for every single size, color, or rule layout creates catalog duplication and breaks stock tracing.

💵

Static Price Stalls

Storing prices directly inside the main items table overwrites historical price records and alters previous billing logs.

📦

Pack Scale Latency

Inefficient mapping of different transaction units (buying in pieces, selling in boxes or cartons) breaks stock balance calculations.

🔒

Audit Inconsistencies

Lacking separated transaction records for suppliers and customers makes inventory auditing and GST reconciliation difficult.

Our Solution

An enterprise-ready normalized PostgreSQL backend

🎨

Product Variant Model

Splits catalog into base models (Item Master) and variants (colors, layouts) with dynamic JSONB attribute fields.

📊

UOM Configuration

Tracks packaging conversions (1 Carton = 120 Pieces) through a structured mapping table.

📅

Isolated Pricing Ledger

Stores pricing history with start and end dates. Preserves past financial reports while allowing future adjustments.

💼

Unified Ledger

Supports both B2C customer sales and B2B supplier orders in a unified table using database checks.

🛡️

EMI Constraint Triggers

Automatically blocks EMI payment selections for orders under ₹2000 at the database engine layer.

🖥️

Asset Management

Tracks workspace hardware distribution, user communication channels, and device maintenance costs.
